The Ghanian culture, like most African cultures, have traditional naming systems. The Ghanian cultural naming system asigns children names, referred to as their Akan Names by taking into account their gender and the day when they were born.

This application takes into account the day of birth, that is, the day of the week when the child was born and their gender to assign them their Akan Names.

Where people are not aware of their day of birth but know their date of birth, the application will use their date of birth and their year of birth and compute their day of birth.

The table below shows the names assigned to children based on their day of birth and gender;

Monday Kwadwo Adwoa
Tuesday Kwabena Abenaa
